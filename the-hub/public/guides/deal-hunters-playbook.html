Luxury Watches

18 tips to find the best watch deals

1

Track Chrono24 Listings Daily

Set up saved searches on Chrono24 for your target models. Price changes happen overnight. Check at the same time daily (9am-10am works best) to spot fresh listings before they get traction.

2

Grey Market vs Authorized Dealer â€” Know the Trade-Offs

Grey market = 20-40% savings but no manufacturer warranty. AD = full warranty, purchase history, but you pay MSRP. For watches under $3k, grey is usually fine. For Rolex/AP/Patek, the AD relationship matters long-term.

3

The "Box & Papers" Premium is Overrated

Watch-only pieces sell for 10-15% less than full-set. But unless you're buying for investment, you're wearing the watch â€” not the box. Save money, skip the packaging drama.

4

Buy Pre-Owned from Reputable Dealers, Not eBay Randos

Use Watchbox, Crown & Caliber, Bob's Watches. They authenticate, offer warranties, and have return policies. That $500 premium over a sketchy eBay listing? It's insurance against a $5,000 fake.

5

Use WatchCharts to Track Historical Prices

Before you buy, check WatchCharts.com to see 6-month price trends. If prices are dropping 5% per month, wait. If they've flatlined, that's your buy signal.

6

Negotiate Everything (Yes, Even at ADs)

ADs will budge on non-Rolex/Patek pieces. Ask for 10-15% off retail on Omega, Tudor, TAG, etc. Say "I'm ready to buy today if we can work on the price." Works 60% of the time.

7

Avoid "Investment Grade" Hype Watches

If a watch influencer is pushing it as "the next Daytona," run. You're late. Real deals are on boring, solid references nobody's hyping â€” Omega Aqua Terra, Tudor BB36, Grand Seiko.

8

Buy During Market Corrections

Stock market crashes? Luxury watch prices follow. When the S&P drops 10%+, used watch listings surge and prices dip. Set aside dry powder for these moments.

9

Join Watch Communities for Early Tips

Subreddits like r/Watchexchange and forums like WatchUSeek have deals before they hit Chrono24. Be active, build reputation, get first dibs on private sales.

10

Ask About Service History

A $5k watch needing a $800 service isn't a $5k watch â€” it's $5,800. Always ask when it was last serviced. If the seller doesn't know or says "never," factor that cost into your offer.

11

Smaller Dealers = Better Deals

Big names like Watchbox have overhead. Small regional dealers (check Google Maps in your city) often have lower prices and will negotiate harder to move inventory.

12

Time Your Purchase for End-of-Quarter

Watch dealers have sales quotas. Last week of March, June, September, December = desperation. They'll take lower margins to hit targets.

13

Avoid Rolex Sports Models If You Want Value

Submariner, GMT, Daytona = 2x-3x retail on grey market. But Rolex Datejust, Oyster Perpetual, Air-King? Near retail or below. Same quality, zero hype tax.

14

Check Japanese Dealers for Global Arbitrage

Sites like Sakura Watches have Grand Seiko, Citizen, Seiko at 20-30% below US retail. Shipping is fast, authentication is solid.

15

Look for Unpopular Dial Colors

Blue dial Omega Seamaster = market price. Champagne dial? Silver dial? Often 15-20% cheaper because they're less "Instagram-friendly." You get the same watch for less.

16

Buy from Private Sellers (with Escrow)

Dealers mark up 25-40%. Private sellers on forums/Reddit just want their money back. Use escrow services like Chrono24 Escrow for protection.

17

Understand the "Birth Year Premium"

Vintage watches from your birth year cost 10-20% more because of sentimental demand. If you're flexible on year, you'll find better prices on neighboring years.

18

We track 1,000+ watch listings daily across Chrono24, eBay, and forums. Get alerts when your grail watch drops below market value. Check deals â†’

Sneakers

17 tips for scoring hyped kicks at reasonable prices

19

StockX vs GOAT â€” Where to Buy

StockX has higher volume = more competitive pricing. GOAT has better authentication + used/defect options. For new hyped drops, check both. For older releases, GOAT wins.

20

Wait 2-3 Weeks After a Hyped Release

Release day = peak panic buying. Within 2 weeks, resellers flood the market and prices drop 20-40%. Unless it's a true unicorn collab (Travis Scott, OFF-WHITE), patience wins.

21

Use Bots? Skip It. Use Monitors Instead.

Sneaker bots cost $500-$2k/year and aren't guaranteed. Free Discord monitors like Sole Retriever, CopSupply alert you to restocks in real-time. Manual copping still works for GRs.

22

Target "Sitting" Dunks and Jordan 1 Lows

Not every Nike drop sells out. Non-OG colorways sit at retailers for weeks. Sign up for store newsletters â€” you'll get 10-20% off codes that stack on already-available inventory.

23

Buy "Used - Like New" on GOAT

"Like New" often means tried on once. You save 15-25% vs deadstock and the shoe is literally identical. People return impulse buys all the time.

24

Size Up or Down for Better Prices

Size 9-10.5 = highest demand = highest resale. If you can fit a 8.5 or 11, those sizes trade 10-15% cheaper. Check brand sizing guides â€” Nike and Adidas fit differently.

25

Follow Sneaker Deal Accounts on Twitter/X

@snkr_twitr, @SoleLinks, @KicksDeals post flash sales, discount codes, and restocks. Turn on notifications. Deals sell out in minutes.

26

Outlet Stores Are Gold Mines

Nike outlets get "hash wall" returns and old stock. Check every visit â€” you'll find Jordan 1s, Dunks, and retro runners for 40-60% off retail. Go on weekdays for best selection.

27

Ignore Instagram Hype â€” Buy What You Like

If you're chasing Yeezy Slides because of TikTok, you're paying the hype tax. Ask yourself: Would I wear this if it was $30 at Foot Locker? If no, skip it.

28

Pre-Order Restocks on Foot Locker / Finish Line

Major retailers restock popular models monthly. Sign up for Foot Locker's FLX rewards â€” members get early access to restocks and releases.

29

Track Prices on StockX Price Chart

Every listing page has a 12-month price history. If prices are trending down, set a bid below current asks. Sellers panic during downtrends and accept lowballs.

30

Buy International Releases on eBay

Some colorways only release in EU/Asia. eBay sellers in UK, Japan, Australia ship globally. Factor in 2-3 week shipping but you'll get exclusive drops at near-retail.

31

Join Raffles for Free â€” Skip Paid "Guaranteed" Services

SNKRS app, END raffles, SNS raffles = free entries. Services charging $50-100 for "guaranteed W" are scams. Enter every free raffle you can find.

32

Avoid Fake Websites â€” Stick to Known Retailers

Scam sites copy Nike/Foot Locker designs and advertise on Google. Only buy from: Nike.com, Adidas.com, StockX, GOAT, Foot Locker, Finish Line, JD Sports, END. If it's too good to be true, it's fake.

33

Sell Your Old Pairs to Fund New Ones

That pair you wore 5 times? Still worth $80-120 on GOAT used section. Clean them up, take good photos, list them. Sneaker rotation should pay for itself.

34

New Balance & ASICS = Underrated Value

Everyone chases Nike/Jordan. Meanwhile, New Balance 550s, 2002Rs, and ASICS Gel-Lyte IIIs sit at retail or below. Comfort is on par, quality is often better.

Cars

15 tips for buying your next car without getting ripped off

36

Use Carvana/CarMax for Baseline Pricing

Check CarMax/Carvana for your target model. Their no-haggle prices = market ceiling. If a dealer is asking more, walk away. If private seller is asking less, that's your sweet spot.

37

Buy Used Cars with 30k-50k Miles

New cars lose 20% value when you drive off the lot. At 30k-50k miles, depreciation slows dramatically. You get a near-new car for 40-50% less than MSRP. That's the value zone.

38

Get Pre-Approved Financing Before Shopping

Credit unions beat dealer financing 80% of the time. Get pre-approved at 5-6% APR, then tell the dealer to beat it. If they can't, you're already set. Never negotiate monthly payment â€” negotiate total price.

39

Shop End-of-Month/End-of-Year for New Cars

Dealers have monthly/quarterly sales targets. Last 3 days of the month = desperation mode. December = best month (double incentives). Expect 8-12% off MSRP on in-stock models.

40

Always Get a Pre-Purchase Inspection

$150 inspection at an independent mechanic saves you from a $5k nightmare. Sellers who refuse inspection are hiding problems. Non-negotiable for used cars.

41

Buy Boring Colors for Better Deals

White, silver, black = highest demand. Beige? Brown? Dark green? Sell for 5-10% less because they're "hard to move." You get the same car, just in a color people ignore on the lot.

42

Avoid Certified Pre-Owned (CPO) Unless Warranty Matters

CPO = 10-15% markup for a warranty. If you're buying a reliable brand (Toyota, Lexus, Honda), skip CPO. Put that $3k toward future repairs instead â€” you'll come out ahead.

43

Check Car History with Carfax + AutoCheck

Sellers provide Carfax. You should also run AutoCheck â€” they pull from different databases. One accident report might show on one but not the other.

44

Negotiate Out-the-Door Price, Not Monthly Payment

Dealers love "What monthly payment are you targeting?" because they can hide fees/interest. Always say: "I care about total out-the-door price. What's your best number?"

45

Private Party > Dealer for Used Cars

Private sellers don't have lot overhead. You save 15-20% vs dealer prices. Use Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, Autotrader. Bring a friend who knows cars + do the PPI.

46

Lease Deals Are Only Good If You Drive <12k Miles/Year

Leases penalize you $0.25-0.30/mile over the cap. If you commute 40+ miles daily, you'll blow past 12k and owe thousands at lease end. Buy used instead.

47

Test Drive Multiple of the Same Model

Not all used cars are equal. Same year/model can drive completely differently based on previous owner care. Test drive 3-4 examples. The best one will be obvious.

48

Say No to Dealer Add-Ons (Paint Protection, Gap Insurance, Extended Warranties)

These are pure profit for dealers. Paint protection = $50 of spray, marked up to $1,500. Gap insurance = buy from your regular insurer for 1/3 the price. Extended warranties rarely pay out.

49

Timing: Buy in Winter, Sell in Spring

Convertibles, sports cars, trucks = highest prices in spring/summer. Sedans = cheapest in winter. If you can wait for off-season, you'll save 10-15%.
