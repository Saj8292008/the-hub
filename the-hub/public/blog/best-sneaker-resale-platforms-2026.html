Authenticated Marketplaces

These platforms verify authenticity before delivery. Best for trust and convenience, but highest fees.

StockX

Founded: 2016 | Detroit, MI

Best For: High-volume sellers, market data enthusiasts, international buyers

Fees:

  • Buyer: 13.5% transaction fee
  • Seller: 8-12% (tiered by volume) + $3 processing
  • Shipping: Free for buyers, seller pays $12-20

Pros:

  • Transparent bid/ask pricing (see full market)
  • Multiple authentication facilities (fast processing)
  • Portfolio tracking for collections
  • Lower fees for high-volume sellers (8% at Level 4)
  • Global shipping to 50+ countries

Cons:

  • Highest buyer fee among competitors
  • High beginner seller fees (12%)
  • Deadstock only (no used market)
  • Customer service issues during peak times

Verdict: Best for serious resellers and buyers who want market visibility.

GOAT

Founded: 2015 | Los Angeles, CA

Best For: Used sneaker market, mobile-first users, casual sellers

Fees:

  • Buyer: 9.5% service fee + $15 shipping
  • Seller: 9.5% commission (flat rate) + $5 cash-out
  • Shipping: Free pre-paid label for sellers

Pros:

  • Only major platform with authenticated used market
  • Best mobile app experience
  • Free shipping labels for sellers
  • Lower buyer fee than StockX
  • See actual photos of used pairs
  • Condition ratings (DS, VNDS, 9/10, etc.)

Cons:

  • Single LA facility = slower authentication
  • $15 shipping fee for buyers
  • No fee reduction for high-volume sellers
  • Less pricing transparency than StockX

Verdict: Best for used sneakers and sellers who value simplicity over volume discounts.

eBay (Authenticity Guarantee)

Founded: 1995 | Authentication launched 2020

Best For: Bargain hunters, fast payouts, vintage finds

Fees:

  • Buyer: $0 (no buyer fee!)
  • Seller: 13.25% final value fee
  • Shipping: Varies by listing (often seller pays)
  • Authentication: Free for sneakers $100+

Pros:

  • Zero buyer fees (huge advantage)
  • Auction format can yield below-market prices
  • Best Offer negotiation
  • Fastest payouts (1-3 days)
  • Vintage/rare pairs not on StockX/GOAT
  • Returns allowed (if seller permits)

Cons:

  • Highest seller fee (13.25%)
  • More work for sellers (photos, descriptions)
  • Authentication only for $100+ sneakers
  • Variable quality (not all sellers are pros)

Verdict: Best for buyers (no fees) and sellers who need immediate cash.

Stadium Goods

Founded: 2015 | New York, NY

Best For: Instant delivery (NYC), rare finds, consignment sellers

Fees:

  • Buyer: 10% service fee + shipping
  • Seller: 20% consignment fee (higher than competitors)
  • Physical store pickup available (NYC)

Pros:

  • Physical retail location (NYC SoHo)
  • Curated rare inventory
  • Instant gratification for NYC buyers
  • Consignment model (they handle everything)

Cons:

  • 20% consignment fee is brutal for sellers
  • Limited inventory vs StockX/GOAT
  • Higher prices (premium positioning)
  • Slower consignment payouts

Verdict: Only for NYC buyers wanting instant pickup or sellers with ultra-rare inventory.

Flight Club

Founded: 2005 | New York, NY (now owned by GOAT)

Best For: NYC/LA physical shopping, legacy brand trust

Fees:

  • Buyer: 10-15% markup (built into price)
  • Seller: 20% consignment
  • Same-day pickup in NYC/LA stores

Pros:

  • OG sneaker consignment shop (legacy reputation)
  • Physical stores (NYC, LA, Miami)
  • Integrated with GOAT (cross-platform inventory)
  • High-end inventory (grails, vintage)

Cons:

  • Same issues as Stadium Goods (20% fee, high prices)
  • Online prices often higher than GOAT direct
  • Limited to major metro areas

Verdict: Visit the store for the experience, but buy on GOAT for better prices.

Peer-to-Peer Platforms

Trade or sell directly to other sneakerheads. Lower fees, but more trust required.

Tradeblock

Founded: 2020

Best For: Trading sneakers without cash, unique swaps

How It Works:

List sneakers you want to trade. Match with others. Each side ships to Tradeblock for verification, then they ship to final destinations.

Fees:

  • $10-20 per trade (depends on value)
  • Much cheaper than selling + buying separately

Pros:

  • Trade without cash (avoid fees on both ends)
  • Authentication included
  • Unique trade opportunities (can't find on marketplaces)
  • Fun app experience (like dating apps for sneakers)

Cons:

  • Smaller user base = fewer matches
  • Requires patience (trades take time)
  • Limited to equal-value trades (or cash + sneaker)

Verdict: Great niche platform if you want to refresh your collection without spending cash.

Poshmark / Mercari

Founded: 2011 (Poshmark), 2013 (Mercari)

Best For: Casual sales, used sneakers, local buyers

Fees:

  • Poshmark: 20% fee ($15+), $2.95 flat (under $15)
  • Mercari: 10-13% selling fee
  • No authentication (buyer beware)

Pros:

  • Easy listing process
  • Built-in shipping labels
  • Good for used/worn sneakers
  • Can bundle multiple items

Cons:

  • No authentication (scam risk)
  • Casual buyers often lowball
  • Return disputes common
  • Not ideal for high-value sneakers

Verdict: Use for worn budget sneakers ($50-150). Avoid for expensive deadstock.

Grailed

Founded: 2013

Best For: Streetwear + sneakers, fashion-forward buyers

Fees:

  • 9% commission (standard)
  • 6% for Premium members ($20/month)
  • PayPal fees additional (~3%)

Pros:

  • Streetwear audience = higher willingness to pay
  • Direct messaging with buyers
  • Curated marketplace (not cluttered)
  • Good for rare/vintage finds

Cons:

  • No authentication (trust-based)
  • More fashion-focused than sneaker-centric
  • Smaller sneaker inventory vs StockX/GOAT

Verdict: Best for high-fashion sneaker collabs (Balenciaga, Rick Owens, Margiela).

International Options

KICKS CREW

Founded: 2008 | Hong Kong

Best For: Asia-Pacific buyers, fast international shipping

Fees:

  • No buyer fees (built into pricing)
  • Free shipping on orders $200+
  • Authentication included

Pros:

  • Excellent international reach (Asia, Australia)
  • Fast shipping from Hong Kong
  • Asia-exclusive releases available
  • Rewards program for frequent buyers

Cons:

  • Prices often 5-10% higher than StockX
  • Customer service can be slow
  • Not ideal for US-based buyers (StockX/GOAT better)

Verdict: Best for buyers outside North America, especially Asia-Pacific.

Klekt

Founded: 2020 | Europe (Germany)

Best For: European buyers and sellers

Fees:

  • Buyer: 5% fee
  • Seller: 10% commission
  • Lower than StockX/GOAT for EU market

Pros:

  • EU-based (avoids customs/VAT issues)
  • Lower fees than US platforms
  • Euro pricing (no currency conversion)

Cons:

  • Smaller inventory than global platforms
  • Limited reach outside Europe

Verdict: Best platform for European sneakerheads (avoid import fees).

Local & Direct Sales

Facebook Marketplace

Fees:

$0 (completely free, cash transactions)

Pros:

  • Zero fees = maximum profit
  • Meet locally, inspect in person
  • Cash transactions (instant payout)
  • Best for finding steals (sellers don't know value)

Cons:

  • No authentication (you must verify yourself)
  • Safety concerns (meet in public places)
  • Flaky buyers/sellers
  • Requires legit-checking skills

Verdict: Best for experienced buyers hunting deals. Not for beginners.

OfferUp / Craigslist

Similar to Facebook Marketplace—zero fees, local meetups, cash deals. Same pros and cons.

Verdict: Use for budget pickups and local flips. Always meet in public.

Instagram / Twitter Direct Sales

Fees:

$0 + payment processor (PayPal/Venmo ~3%)

Pros:

  • Build personal brand and following
  • Direct customer relationships
  • Control your pricing and presentation
  • Best margins (minimal fees)

Cons:

  • Requires building trust and following
  • Time-intensive (customer service, DMs)
  • Scam risk (fake buyers, chargebacks)
  • No buyer protection = harder to sell high-ticket items

Verdict: Advanced strategy for established resellers building a brand.

How to Choose the Right Platform

Choose StockX if:

  • You're selling 50+ pairs per year (level up for lower fees)
  • You want full market transparency and data
  • You're shipping internationally
  • You only deal in deadstock

Choose GOAT if:

  • You're buying or selling used sneakers
  • You prefer mobile shopping
  • You're a casual seller (under 50 sales/year)
  • You want to see actual photos of pairs

Choose eBay if:

  • You're a buyer who wants zero fees
  • You need money in 1-3 days (fastest payouts)
  • You're hunting for auction deals
  • You're selling vintage/rare pairs not on other platforms

Choose Facebook/Local if:

  • You know how to legit check sneakers
  • You want maximum profit (zero fees)
  • You're hunting steals from uninformed sellers
  • You're comfortable meeting strangers in public

The Smart Multi-Platform Approach

Most successful resellers don't pick one—they use multiple platforms strategically:

  • Buy: Facebook (steals), eBay (no buyer fees), GOAT used (discounts)
  • Sell deadstock: StockX (if high volume), GOAT (if casual)
  • Sell used: GOAT (authenticated) or Facebook (local, cash)
  • Quick cash: eBay (1-3 day payouts)
  • Trade: Tradeblock (avoid buy/sell fees)

Example workflow: Find Jordan 1 "Chicago" on Facebook for $450 cash. Legit check passes. List on StockX for $520 (current market). After 10% fee, net $425. Lost money? No—you saved 13.5% buyer fee ($70) by buying on Facebook. Total arbitrage profit: $45 risk-free.

Bottom Line

Quick Recommendations:

Best all-around for beginners:

GOAT (simple, authenticated, fair fees)

Best for serious resellers:

StockX (level up to 8% fees, market data)

Best for buyers:

eBay (zero buyer fees) or Facebook (cash deals)

Best for used sneakers:

GOAT (only authenticated used market)

Best for fast cash:

eBay (1-3 day payouts)

Best for international:

KICKS CREW (Asia), Klekt (Europe), StockX (global)

There's no single "best" platform—it depends on your goals, volume, and location. Start with one authenticated platform (GOAT or StockX) to learn the ropes. Once comfortable, expand to eBay for buying and Facebook for hunting deals. Over time, you'll develop your own multi-platform strategy that maximizes profit.
