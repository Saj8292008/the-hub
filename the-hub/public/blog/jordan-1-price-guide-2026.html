Market Overview: Jordan 1 in 2026

The Jordan 1 market has matured significantly since the 2020-2021 hype peak. Here's what's changed:

What's Up

  • OG colorways: Chicago, Bred, Royal continue climbing (averaging +8-12% annually)
  • Travis Scott collabs: Still holding strong, minimal depreciation
  • Vintage originals: 1985 pairs now $5,000+ in wearable condition
  • Quality retros: "Lost and Found" aged leather style proved popular

What's Down

  • Mid and Low variants: Oversupply killed resale on most (now retail or below)
  • General Release Highs: Too many colorways, not enough demand
  • Women's exclusives: Price gap narrowing vs men's (used to be 40% cheaper, now 20%)
  • Patent leather versions: Market fatigue with shiny finishes

Key Insight:

In 2026, only about 15% of Jordan 1 releases are actually profitable to flip. The market rewards OG colorways, limited collabs, and quality materials. Everything else sits or sells below retail. Choose wisely.

Tier 5: Below Retail (Under $175)

These sit on shelves or sell below box price on resale sites. Avoid for resale; good for personal wear.

  • Most Jordan 1 Mids: $100-150 (oversaturated, no resale)
  • General Release Lows: $90-140 (except Travis collabs)
  • Women's GR Highs: $120-170 (too many colorways, demand can't keep up)
  • Team colorways: $85-130 (college themes rarely resell)
  • Patent leather GRs: $110-160 (market fatigued on shiny)

Exception: If you find any of these at steep discount (40%+ off retail) at outlets, they're worth buying to flip locally or on eBay. Someone will buy a $120 Jordan 1 Mid for $80.

What to Avoid

  • Jordan 1 Mids: Never appreciate. Buy to wear, not invest.
  • Recent GR Highs: Too many colorways dilute value. Pass unless deep discount.
  • Hype collabs past peak: Travis x Fragment dropped 40% from ATH. Avoid buying tops.
  • Women's exclusives (most): Market still undervalues despite narrowing gap.

Portfolio Strategy

If you're building a sneaker investment portfolio, allocate like this:

  • 40% Blue Chips: 2015 OGs (Chicago, Bred, Royal) — stable, steady growth
  • 30% High-Grade Collabs: Travis, Off-White, Union — cultural staying power
  • 20% Emerging Plays: Lost and Found, A Ma Maniére — undervalued now, big upside
  • 10% Wildcards: New releases with potential (recent boutique collabs, quality drops)

Time horizon: Think 3-5 years minimum. Sneakers aren't stocks—liquidity is lower, and short-term flipping erodes profit with fees. Buy what you believe in and hold.

Bottom Line

Key Takeaways:

  • OG colorways win: Chicago, Bred, Royal, Shadow hold/appreciate best
  • Collabs age well: Travis, Off-White, Union, Fragment stay valuable
  • Quality matters: Leather quality and materials impact long-term value
  • Mids and Lows struggle: Except Travis Lows, don't expect resale
  • Oversupply kills value: Too many GR Highs = most sell at/below retail
  • Buy the dip: Lost and Found is currently undervalued (contrarian play)

The Jordan 1 market rewards patience and selectivity. Only ~15% of releases are worth flipping. The rest are shoes to wear and enjoy—which is fine! Not everything needs to be an investment.
