## Suggested Talking Points (Adapt Per Podcast)

### Universal Topics (Work Everywhere)
- **Origin story:** Why I built The Hub ‚Äî the pain point, the aha moment
- **Data insights:** Surprising patterns from marketplace listings (be specific)
- **How aggregation works:** Behind-the-scenes of pulling data from multiple sources
- **Collector psychology:** Why people overpay, impulse buy, chase hype
- **Red flags and scams:** How to spot fake listings, too-good-to-be-true deals
- **Market trends:** What's hot, what's cooling off, what's undervalued (with examples)

### Watch Podcast-Specific
- The vintage watch minefield: Authentication, condition, seller trust
- Why micro-brands are booming (and which ones to watch)
- Rolex math: Does it ever make sense to pay over retail?
- The "gateway watch" phenomenon: How collectors evolve their tastes

### Sneaker Podcast-Specific
- Bots vs. humans: The state of sneaker resale in 2026
- Regional price differences: Why EU vs. US prices vary wildly
- The death of hype: Are people finally buying what they like vs. what flips?
- Authenticity anxiety: How to buy with confidence in the secondary market

### Car Podcast-Specific
- Bring a Trailer effect: Has BaT changed classic car values permanently?
- Finding cars before they hit BaT (and why that matters)
- The "drive it or flip it" dilemma: Collector vs. investor mindset
- Seasonal patterns: When classic car prices dip (and when to pounce)

### Reseller / Business Podcast-Specific
- Niche aggregation as a business model: Why focus beats broad
- Monetization strategies: Ads, affiliate, premium ‚Äî what works
- Technical challenges: Scraping, data cleaning, legal gray areas
- Scaling: How to go from weekend project to real business

## Podcast-Specific Customizations

### For Watch Podcasts (Grey NATO, Worn & Wound, TBWS):
- Emphasize the collecting journey, not just the tech
- Talk about specific models you've tracked/found
- Reference the hosts' watch collections or recent episodes
- Keep it enthusiast-focused, less about business

### For Sneaker Podcasts (Sneaker Podcast, Full Size Run, Locked In):
- Bring energy and humor ‚Äî sneaker culture is fun
- Have specific sneaker stories ready (grails, steals, fails)
- Acknowledge the resale chaos and bot wars
- Be self-aware about being a "tech guy in sneaker world"

### For Car Podcasts (Spike's Car Radio, Smoking Tire):
- Lead with passion for cars, not just data
- Have 2-3 favorite car finds ready to discuss
- Reference specific cars the hosts have driven/featured
- Don't oversell the tech ‚Äî car people want stories

### For Business Podcasts (Side Hustle Show, My First Million):
- Focus on the business model and growth
- Be transparent about challenges and mistakes
- Share numbers (revenue, traffic, costs) if possible
- Frame it as "here's what I learned" not "here's how great I am"

## Pre-Interview Prep Checklist

Before recording, prepare:
- [ ] 3-5 interesting data points/stories you can reference
- [ ] Your "origin story" (why you built The Hub) ‚Äî keep it under 2 min
- [ ] 2-3 examples of crazy deals you've found (with specifics)
- [ ] 1-2 funny or surprising stories from the marketplace world
- [ ] Questions you want to ask the host (turn it into a conversation)
- [ ] Your "call to action" (what you want listeners to do ‚Äî visit the site, check out a feature, etc.)
- [ ] Test your mic/internet connection
- [ ] Have water nearby (long interviews = dry mouth)

## Post-Interview Follow-Up

After recording:
1. **Thank the host** ‚Äî Email within 24 hours expressing gratitude
2. **Share the episode** ‚Äî Post to your social media when it drops, tag the podcast
3. **Engage with listeners** ‚Äî If people comment/tweet, respond thoughtfully
4. **Track results** ‚Äî Monitor traffic spikes to The Hub from the podcast
5. **Build the relationship** ‚Äî Stay in touch with the host, offer to come back if there's interest
