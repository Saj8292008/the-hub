## Strategic Takeaways

### 1. **Implement Tiered Subscription Model**
**Priority: HIGH**

WatchCharts' $199-$899/year tiers show clear demand for premium features. The Hub should offer:
- **Free tier:** Basic deal alerts, limited history
- **Pro tier ($99-149/year):** Unlimited alerts, 1-year price history, ad-free, priority notifications
- **Elite tier ($299-399/year):** Multi-year price history, deal forecasting, API access, custom alerts

**Why it works:** 
- Subscription revenue is predictable and compounds
- Users who pay stay engaged longer
- Clear upgrade path from free to paid

**Implementation:**
- Start with simple Pro tier first
- Use Stripe for billing
- Offer annual discount (save 20% vs monthly)

---

### 2. **Build Price History & Forecasting for Watches**
**Priority: HIGH**

WatchCharts' price forecasting and 15-year historical data are killer features. The Hub should:
- Scrape and store historical prices for all watch deals
- Build simple trend graphs (7-day, 30-day, 90-day, 1-year)
- Add "Price Drop Alert" when item hits new low
- Implement ML-based "Deal Score" (0-100) ranking quality
- Show "Last seen at this price: 6 months ago" context

**Why it works:**
- Builds trust ("Is this actually a good deal?")
- Unique value vs. aggregators that just show current price
- Creates moat (requires historical data investment)

**Implementation:**
- PostgreSQL table for price snapshots (daily)
- Lightweight ML model for deal scoring
- Chart.js or Recharts for visualization
