## Growth Channels (Prioritized)

### Channel 1: Reddit (HIGH PRIORITY)
**Why:** Watch enthusiasts hang out there, they trust peer recommendations

**Strategy:**
1. **Build karma first** (1-2 weeks)
   - Comment genuinely on r/Watches, r/WatchExchange
   - Share expertise, help people
   - Build credibility

2. **Post high-value deals** (Week 3+)
   - Only post genuinely good deals (our best finds)
   - Add value in comments (context, pricing history, etc.)
   - Subtle CTA: "I send these to my newsletter if you want more"

3. **Measure & iterate**
   - Track: clicks, signups, feedback
   - Adjust posting frequency/format based on response

**Target:** 20-30 subscribers from Reddit in first month

### Channel 2: Telegram Channel (@hubtest123)
**Why:** Real-time deal alerts work great on Telegram

**Strategy:**
1. **Post deals automatically** (implement this week)
   - Hot deals get auto-posted
   - Include price, images, link
   - "Subscribe to newsletter for weekly digests"

2. **Promote the channel**
   - Reddit signature/profile
   - Twitter bio
   - Cross-promote with newsletter

3. **Build community**
   - Let people discuss deals in comments
   - Feature member finds
   - Create engagement

**Target:** 100 followers by end of Q1

### Channel 3: Twitter/X Bot
**Why:** Reach + virality potential

**Strategy:**
1. **Auto-post hot deals** (@TheHubDeals)
   - Image of watch/sneaker
   - Price + context
   - Link to details
   - Hashtags: #WatchDeals #WatchCollector

2. **Engage with community**
   - Reply to deal seekers
   - Retweet good finds from others
   - Build authority

**Target:** 500 followers, 5-10 signups per month

### Channel 4: Direct Outreach (LATER)
**Why:** Higher quality, more engaged subscribers

**Strategy:**
- Find watch/sneaker YouTubers/bloggers
- Offer early access or custom alerts
- Build partnerships

**Target:** 10-15 high-quality subscribers

## Key Insights

1. **Distribution > Product** right now
   - We have the machine, need the audience
   - Focus 80% on growth, 20% on product

2. **Community-first approach**
   - Build trust before asking for signups
   - Provide value upfront
   - Be genuinely helpful

3. **Quality > Quantity**
   - 10 engaged subscribers > 100 random emails
   - Only share truly good deals
   - Reputation is everything

## Risks & Mitigation

**Risk:** Reddit mods think we're spamming
- **Mitigation:** Build karma first, follow rules, provide real value

**Risk:** No one wants another newsletter
- **Mitigation:** Make ours genuinely useful, not just promotion

**Risk:** We run out of good deals to share
- **Mitigation:** Expand to more sources, improve scoring
