## Performance: Identical Where It Matters

### Shared Specifications

**Engine:** 3.0L S58 twin-turbo inline-6
**Horsepower:** 473 hp (base) / 503 hp (Competition)
**Torque:** 406 lb-ft (base) / 479 lb-ft (Competition)
**0-60 mph:** 3.8s (Competition xDrive)
**Top speed:** 155 mph (limited) / 180 mph (M Driver's Package)

**The truth:** M3 and M4 are mechanically identical. Same engine, transmission, differential, suspension, brakes.

### Minor Performance Differences

**M3 advantages:**
- 100 lbs heavier (better planted feel at high speed)
- Slightly longer wheelbase (more stable in corners)
- Better weight distribution with rear passengers

**M4 advantages:**
- 100 lbs lighter (slightly better acceleration)
- Shorter wheelbase (more agile turn-in)
- Lower center of gravity (coupe roof structure)

**Real-world impact:** 0.1 seconds difference in 0-60. You won't notice.

## Driving Experience: Subtle Differences

### Steering Feel

**M3:** Slightly more isolation (due to extra weight and sound deadening)
**M4:** Marginally more direct (lighter weight, stiffer chassis)

**Real-world:** Both have excellent steering. Difference is placebo-level.

### Road Noise

**M3:** Quieter at highway speeds (more insulation)
**M4:** Slightly louder (sportier feel, less sound deadening)

**Impact:** M3 is better for road trips, M4 feels more raw and engaging.

### Visibility

**M3:** Worse rear visibility (sedan design, smaller rear window)
**M4:** Better all-around visibility (coupe greenhouse design)

**Practical note:** Both have blind spot monitoring. Not a dealbreaker either way.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is the M4 faster than the M3?**
A: Barely. The M4 is 100 lbs lighter, giving it a 0.1 second advantage in 0-60 mph tests. In real-world driving, they're identical.

**Q: Does the M3 handle worse because it's heavier?**
A: No. The weight difference (100 lbs) is negligible. Both cars have the same suspension, tires, and chassis dynamics. Professional drivers can't tell the difference on track.

**Q: Is the M4 convertible worth the extra $8,500?**
A: Only if you live in a warm climate and love open-air driving. The convertible is 300 lbs heavier, flexier chassis, and loses trunk space. Most buyers regret convertibles after 6 months.

**Q: Should I get the Competition package?**
A: Yes, if you plan to track the car or want maximum performance. The carbon bucket seats, upgraded suspension, and 30 extra hp are worth $8,000. Skip it if you prioritize comfort.

**Q: Is xDrive worth the $3,500?**
A: If you live in snow/rain climates, yes. xDrive makes the car usable year-round and is 0.3s faster 0-60. However, RWD is more fun and engaging for spirited driving.

**Q: Which holds value better, M3 or M4?**
A: M3 holds value 1-2% better due to wider buyer pool (families need 4 doors). Both depreciate similarly, losing 35-40% in 5 years.

**Q: Should I buy the manual transmission?**
A: Yes, if you enjoy shifting. Manual M3/M4s are future collectibles (last generation with manual option). DCT is faster and easier in traffic, but manual is more engaging and will hold value better.

**Q: Can I daily drive an M3 or M4?**
A: Absolutely. Both have comfortable ride modes, decent fuel economy (22 mpg highway), and usable trunk space. The M3 is slightly more comfortable for daily use due to better rear seat.
