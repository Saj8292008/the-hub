## Authentication Guide

**Critical:** Fakes are 40%+ of resale market.

### Quick Authentication Checks

**1. Swoosh Quality**
- Real: Sharp edges, consistent curve, proper placement
- Fake: Soft edges, inconsistent curve, wrong angle

**2. Wings Logo**
- Real: Embossed (raised), "R" points to top lace hole
- Fake: Printed (flat), "R" misaligned

**3. Toebox Perforations**
- Real: Uniform size/spacing, clean holes
- Fake: Irregular size, messy punching

**4. Stitching**
- Real: Tight, consistent, no loose threads
- Fake: Loose, uneven, fraying

**5. Smell**
- Real: Leather/glue smell (neutral)
- Fake: Chemical/plastic smell (strong)

**Use legit check services:**
- CheckCheck App: $3/check
- Legitmark: $10-30
- Local consignment shops: $20-50

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What's the most valuable Jordan 1 colorway?**
A: 1985 Chicago original in deadstock condition ($25k+). For modern retros, 2015 Chicago ($1,800-2,200) and Off-White Chicago 2017 ($4,000-5,500).

**Q: Are Jordan 1s a good investment?**
A: OG colorways (Chicago, Bred, Royal) appreciate 10-15% annually. Limited collabs (Travis Scott, Off-White) appreciate 15-25%. General releases rarely appreciate.

**Q: Which size Jordan 1 is most valuable?**
A: Size 10-11 command highest prices (most demand). Smaller (< 9) and larger (13+) sizes sell for 20-35% less.

**Q: Where should I buy Jordan 1s?**
A: StockX/GOAT for safety and liquidity. eBay for deals with authentication guarantee. Avoid Facebook/Craigslist unless you can authenticate yourself.

**Q: How can I tell if Jordan 1s are fake?**
A: Check swoosh quality, wings logo embossing, toebox perforations, stitching consistency, and smell. Use CheckCheck app ($3) or legit check service ($10-30) for authentication.

**Q: Should I buy used or deadstock Jordan 1s?**
A: Deadstock for investment/resale value. Used for personal wear (save 30-50%). Lightly used (8/10 condition) best value for wearers.

**Q: Do Jordan 1 Lows have resale value?**
A: Much less than highs. Jordan 1 Lows trade near retail ($100-150) except rare collabs. Stick to highs for investment.

**Q: What's the best Jordan 1 under $300?**
A: Court Purple 2.0 ($280-340), Heritage ($240-300), or Hyper Royal ($280-350). All solid colorways with moderate appreciation potential.
